Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 18, 2026

The Short Version

We store your email and the UUIDs you generate. That's literally it. We're not Facebook.

1. Information We Collect

When you use UUIDaaS, we collect the following groundbreaking data:

  • Your email address - Because we need to associate your UUIDs with something
  • UUIDs you generate - The entire purpose of this service
  • Timestamps - So we know when you generated that UUID at 3am
  • Your IP address - For rate limiting (3 signups/hour, remember?)

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your data for the following revolutionary purposes:

  • To generate UUIDs for you (shocking, we know)
  • To prevent you from signing up 47 times
  • To count how many UUIDs you've generated (metrics!)
  • To make sure you're not a bot generating UUIDs for nefarious purposes

3. Data Storage

Your data is stored in Turso, which is SQLite in the cloud. Yes, we went full circle from local SQLite to cloud SQLite. Your UUIDs are backed up across multiple regions, ensuring that future archaeologists will be able to see exactly what UUIDs you generated.

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell your data. We do not share your data. Honestly, who would want to buy a list of UUIDs and email addresses? If someone offers us money for this data, we'll probably laugh and then say no.

5. Your Rights

You have the following rights:

  • Right to access - You can see your own user info via the API
  • Right to be forgotten - Coming soon (when we implement the delete endpoint)
  • Right to generate more UUIDs - As long as you stay under the rate limit

6. Security

We hash your API keys with SHA-256. Is this as secure as bcrypt? No. Is it sufficient for a joke UUID service? Also no, but here we are. Your API key is shown exactly once during signup. If you lose it, you'll need to... actually we don't have password reset yet. Don't lose it.

7. Cookies

We don't use cookies. This isn't a web app, it's an API. The only thing we're tracking is how many times you've hit the rate limit.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy if we add more over-engineered features. If we do, the "Last updated" date at the top will change, which is your notification.

9. Contact

If you have privacy concerns about a UUID generation service, please reconsider your life choices. But if you must, you can reach us by generating a UUID and hoping we notice.

TL;DR: We have your email and your UUIDs. We're not doing anything evil with them. We're literally just storing them in a database. Relax.
